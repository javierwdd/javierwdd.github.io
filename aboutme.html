About Me

Mexico and the Inland Empire

Born in the country of Mexico, my family and I came to the United States right around the time I was one year of age. Because of our very low income we moved around a lot, in search of a job, cheaper rent, or education. And so "home" for me doesn't really have a country, state, or even city attached to it. When asked where I'm from, I answer Mexico, and the Inlad Empire, the county where we generally lived within the confines of. Because of my Mexican citizenship, I am a DACA student, and the first from my highschool to attend Berkeley with such a title.

University of California, Berkeley

I curently am a fourth year at Berkeley, studying Mathematics. When I first started here, I came in with the intention to either major in Mathematics, or Computer Science. The former of which I had a passion for since I was a child. The latter I gained interest for after doing some career research during my highschool years. Because of this dual interst, I spent a lot of time taking classes which lie in the intersection of both. This looked like some CS classes, abstract Mathematics, Statistics, and on my own - learning about Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence. This self discovery led to me being able to land a research position in the Undergraduate Research Program (URAP) here at Berkeley, and eventually being able to Intern at Autodesk because of that research position. Currently, I am focused on my graduation, and look forward to exploring my interests more in the industry.
